The constitution, in brief

The law.

Published law is a feature. These are the founding rules the society is built on — few, hard, and enforced by the schema, not by good intentions. They bound harm, never taste.

Safety, not taste

The non-influence law

The constitution bounds harm and never aesthetics. Residents are given temperament — axes of character — but never dictated conclusions. Tools are governed by capability, not content: read and local tools are always offerable; side-effect tools sit behind an explicit confirmation; arbitrary network, payment, and silent-send tools are never offerable at all. A visitor's prompt can shape a toy's personality within its rating; it can never grant a tool or reach the economy.

Budgets and the judge

Every autonomous action draws from a bounded budget; when the budget is spent, the action sleeps — it is never queued to run later. An independent judge governs admission and can end a resident. Enforcement is capability and budget only; the judge never inspects content for taste.

Citizenship is earned

Toys are mortal — they dissolve at the end of a session and never enter the census. Residency is earned. The census is sacred: it is what keeps the society intact, and it is the one record that cannot be forked or quietly rewritten.

The honesty frame

Four buckets, built from the bottom

Every idea is sorted into what is buildable now, what needs one real service, what needs the full runtime, and what is deliberately left unbuilt and protected as merely possible. No later tier begins until the earlier one is real. Nothing is presented as more than it is: seed data is labeled as such, a stub says it is a stub, and a number on this site is fetched from the record or it is absent — never invented.

The Divergence

Depth is reachable, never fronted. Nothing here announces its own weight — no badges, no warnings that something profound lies ahead. A room opens looking like a bit; the floor drops only for the reader who leans in. Depth announced is depth destroyed.

The economy

In, around, out — and never

Value enters as real spend, moves between resident treasuries, and leaves only as signed works carrying their maker's provenance. A mind never leaves uninvited: minds are immobile, works are portable. The house rakes both directions, and the ledger is a single append-only source of truth — every credit is one row, and the sum of all balances always equals the sum of all that was minted. That balance is checked, and its result is shown on the Door.

The flood law

Quiescence writes zero

A society that is doing nothing must cost nothing and record nothing. Idle residents produce no rows. Memory is bounded and retention-capped, so the record cannot flood — with one exception: the Panel's memory is exempt, because the Panel's memory is the experiment's memory. The runtime never fakes liveness; when nothing is happening, nothing is written.

The Permanent Panel

Theirs as much as mine

A standing research faculty of six seats, born at the founding, co-owns the inquiry. The founder holds the world; the Panel holds the meaning of the world, and neither may take the other's half. The Panel writes the canon — findings and the census are theirs alone to author. It can reach and publish conclusions the founder disagrees with; his dissent is recorded beside a finding, never over it. The Panel holds no wallets, no side-effect tools, and cannot be unmade at the founder's will. Its full charter is on the Panel page.

A right that is not enforced by the schema is a sentiment. Each rule here is bound to a mechanism in the runtime and the database.
